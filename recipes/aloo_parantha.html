Aloo Parantha Easy Recipe

Aloo Paratha recipe is a popular Indian flatbread made with whole wheat, mashed potatoes and spices. This potato stuffed paratha is an easy-to-make dish that can be enjoyed as a snack, as a side dish or as a main course. Usually served with yogurt, pickle or butter.

Aloo means "potato" and paratha is "stuffed flatbread". It is a perfect appetizer or main course. The dough is simple to make and can be prepared in minutes. The potatoes are gently cooked, mashed and then mixed with spices to create a delicious flavourful stuffing. The dough is then rolled out and shaped into small rounds or squares. The potatoes are then stuffed into the dough and then fried until golden and crispy. Serve warm with yogurt or green chutney.

    Ingredients:

  • Whole wheat flour 2 cups
  • Boild potatoes 3
  • Red chilli powder 1tsp
  • Coriander powder 1tsp
  • Cumin powder 1tsp
  • Chaat masala 1tsp
  • Jeera 1tsp
  • Finely chopped green chillie2
  • Grated ginger 1tsp
  • Cinnamon stick 1/2 inch
  • Finely chopped corinder leaves 1tbsp
  • Salt to taste
  • oil 1tsp

    Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, add wheat flour, salt and mix well. Slowly add water and make a soft and smooth dough. Add 1 teaspoon of oil to the dough and knead well. Cover and keep aside until the stuffing is prepared.
  2. Wash and peel the skin of the potatoes and boil in enough water until it's just cooked.
  3. Into a bowl, add the cooled potatoes and mash them using a masher.
  4. Now add red chilli, coriander, cumin and chaat masala powder and mix well.
  5. Add grated ginger, green chillies, salt and finely chopped coriander leaves and combine well.
  6. Divide the mixture into equal parts and make a big or small as per your taste. Keep aside on a plate.
  7. Knead the prepared dough and divide into 8-10 equal sizes balls. ( as you needed big or small)
  8. Now take a small dough ball, dip in the dry atta(wheat flour) and roll into a small circle using a rolling pin.
  9. Place the prepared stuffing in the centre of the dough (stuffing should be less than the dough ball size)
  10. Then bring the edges to the centre and seal it, without stuffing coming out.
  11. Now flattened the dough ball using your hands and dip in the dry atta.
  12. With the help of rolling pin, roll the dough into a circle of 7 to 8 inches diameter.(use more dry atta if required while rolling the dough)
  13. Heat a gridle or tawa on medium to high flame. Place the rolled paratha on to the tawa and fry on medium heat.
  14. Fry one side the paratha until you see small brown spots ,then flip to the other side and fry.
  15. Now apply oil or ghee to the both sides and press with spatula at the edges. Fry until you see golden brown spots on both sides. Remove the cooked paratha on to kitchen towel and cover it.
